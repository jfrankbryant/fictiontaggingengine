“Not if we can help it.” Logan made an aborted motion towards Daken’s shoulder; stilled, likely recalling Daken’s reaction the last time he’d attempted something like that; and folded his hands in his lap.

“You’re a shit liar, Logan.”

“I’m not lyin’.” Logan shut his eyes and sighed. “Alright, Reed realized that studying my healing factor is useless because it’s constantly battling the adamantium. And you don’t have adamantium. The one with less strain on the healing factor, like you, is -”

“Your clone,” Daken breathed. “Laura.”

“Yeah. She’s down in the lab. Reed will come up with something real quick.”

“I doubt he’ll make it in time.” All right. He’d make his peace with this. That was why he’d died the other times too: because they hadn’t thought about that. Better luck next time. “I’m going to die tonight.”

“Don’t say that. It’s gonna be alright.”

Daken hummed. “Yes, it will be. I’ll make sure of it.”

“Yeah.” Logan sighed. “Son, I… heard about what you did to Storm. Saw him, actually. He looked -”

Daken looked away, chest aching. “I don’t want to talk about it.”

“I heard about what you told Reed,” Logan went on, relentless. “That you’re a mess and you need to push people away...”

“It’s what you’ve always known, no?” Daken stared at the ceiling. “That I’m fucked up. A disappointment.”

He could still hear the pain in Logan’s voice from a few loops ago, when Daken had let the bombs explode without warning anyone. His eyes as Daken constantly confirmed his worst fears.

“Son, I -”

“You were right,” Daken said. “I am a mess. So entangled with so much hurt. I don’t want to feel like that anymore. I don’t want to be like that anymore.”

“Is that why you turned to drugs?” Logan questioned softly, a sliver of pity that made Daken feel on edge.

“Don’t give me grief over my coping mechanisms, Logan, you don’t fare much better. You’re a drunkard.”

Logan only let out a laugh. It came out strangled. “Well, you’re right. And I don’t have all the answers. But I can tell you that you don’t have to push people away. If you grow, if you get better, if you’re really set on it -”

“It’s too late,” Daken interrupted him. “And you and I will never be anything either, Logan. There’s just too fucking much grief between us. I did terrible things to you. And you -” He shut his eyes. “You took my revenge from me.”

Logan remained silent, for a moment. Then: “Son,” he exhaled, “I did it to protect you -”

“I know.” Daken squeezed his eyes shut. Protect him from himself; from what he would become, drunk with power. Quod sum eris. A monster. “But I needed that, Logan. I needed to kill him. You can’t understand. It’s my fault that you can’t,” he preempted Logan’s protests. “I never told you anything.”

“I understand that he hurt you,” Logan said fiercely, his eyes flashing. “I understand wanting revenge. But I know how revenge fucks people up, son. I wanted to let you be able to build yourself up from something other than hurt -”

“But it wasn’t your decision to make!” Daken rounded on him, the motion too fast; he broke into a fit of painful coughs. Logan motioned for him and it all came back, all that rage, that blinding rage that had made Daken hate him with every fiber of his being, that had made him want to leave Logan with nothing. “Don’t touch me -”

Something ruptured in his chest. Alarms blared as he coughed up his lungs. Was he going? Was it time? God, he was glad that he was dying. This conversation was too much; he wasn’t ready for it. Perhaps he’d never be.

He registered, faintly, Reed coming unto the room, shouting at Logan to get out of the way. He felt his father’s gaze on him, piercing and hurt and terrified and so fucking determined. He didn’t want Logan to see him die but he didn’t have enough strength to push him away now. He shouldn’t have begun this conversation.

Then he couldn’t think anymore because his whole body clenched, as if he was being squeezed, and he vomited blood on the covers. A fountain of blood. It wasn't stopping.

“Reed!” Logan shouted. “Do something!”

He shouldn't bother.

Logan kept shouting. Too late. Ah, too late. Better luck next time.

The last thing he saw was the door, filled with people coming in, and Johnny, Johnny -

He didn’t want Johnny to see him die…
